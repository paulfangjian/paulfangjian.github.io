Pitch, Yaw and Roll

2018-03-20

I was always confused about the meaning of pitch, yaw and roll when reading papers. As a person who lack of the conception of three-dimensional, I finally understand them after I saw the following description and I would like to share it with you.
Assume you are a plane and flying forward horizontally, so the right behind of yourself is the negative direction of the z axis, above is postive direction of y axis, and right is postive direction of x axis.
As shown in figure: pitch represents the angle you fly towards up or down, imagine you are parachuting from the plane, so the pitch angle is 90. And if are turning left or right, the yaw angle will change. If a thunder strike on the plane and cause it upside down (just for describe this problem, I beg safe for all the plane), roll angle will be 180.
Hope I have already explained it clearly.
